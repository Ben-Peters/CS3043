Directors Intent

Michael Bay put a lot of effort into making the robots in Transformers look complex. Many fans of the original Transformers animated series were unhappy about the look of the new Transformers and consequently Bay recieved a lot of backlash and even death threats []. Bay stuck to his own idea of what Transformers should look like on the big screen because he believed that simply blowing up the cartoon characters would be “the equivalent of ‘Ghostbusters’ with the Marshmallow man” []. However, despite their mechanical appearance, Michael Bay’s Transformers were still able to convey emotions through their facial expressions. In an interview, Bay said he gave the Transformers lips because “we did a lot of facial studies, and emotion is so hard without that kind of movement” []. An important aspect of Transformers is the emotional connection between the Autobots and the human characters, Sam and Mikaela. The anthropomorphic form of the Transformers plays an important role in strengthening the audience’s emotional connection to the Autobots and making Sam’s friendship with the Autobots more convincing.
